This will be a paragrpah about me. I'm not quite sure exactly what I should write here yet, but hopefully the end product will be somewhat eloquent and correctly spelled. If I'm honest, coding definitely isn't my strongest suit...or in my top 50 suits. It isn't particularly enjoyable to me, and I wonder how emotionally traumatized my coding friends might be. I'm too sensitive of a person to handle the largely downhill rollercoaster to find much worthy payoff in the few moments of satisfaction from finally getting a relatively small feature to display properly. Especially since that fleeting moment is often sandwiched by the humiliation of figuring out you missed a semicolon that would've otherwise allowed your code to work an hour ago. I'll happily leave the coding to those who are much hardier in emotional nature than myself. However, I've been and will continue to try my best to learn, and I will whole-heartedly admit that those few instances of genuinely figureing something out is actually rather rewarding.
